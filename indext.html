CMSC 57 - Discrete Mathematics

Just like a great movie catalog explores different genres, my CMSC 57 journey was a collection of fascinating mathematical “features” — each one offering a unique perspective on problem solving, logic, and structure. This site showcases my learning through the lens of a watchlist, creatively presenting topics that shaped my appreciation for Discrete Mathematics.

Counting was our introduction to mathematical precision — where we explored permutations, combinations, and the pigeonhole principle. These tools helped us tackle problems that asked, “How many ways?” — from simple arrangements to complex probability questions.

Algebraic Structures and Groups revealed the power of mathematical symmetry. Like a movie's underlying theme that ties every scene together, group theory showed how sets and operations can follow beautiful, logical patterns with far-reaching applications, especially in cryptography and computer science.

Discrete Probability added a layer of uncertainty — the thrill of not knowing what will happen next. It reminded me of plot twists in a film. Using concepts like Bayes' Theorem, we learned how to make informed guesses and reason under uncertainty, which is crucial in fields like AI and data science.

Graphs were the grand finale — a perfect blend of visuals and logic. I was especially fascinated by Hamiltonian paths and Eulerian circuits. Whether it's finding the shortest route in a delivery network or designing puzzles and mazes, graph theory made me realize how mathematics powers the hidden structures of real-life systems.

Through these topics, I've come to see Discrete Mathematics not just as a subject, but as a powerful toolkit for understanding patterns, solving problems, and telling the hidden stories behind everyday systems, much like the narratives of our favorite films.
