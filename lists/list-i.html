I. COUNTING

Learning about Counting was surprisingly fun and eye-opening. At first, it seemed like it would just be about simple numbers, but I was amazed at how basic counting principles could solve really complex problems. It felt like unlocking hidden shortcuts in problem-solving.

One of the most fascinating parts was the Pigeonhole Principle. It was so cool to see how something that sounds so simple for example like "if you have more pigeons than holes, at least one hole has more than one pigeon", could lead to powerful conclusions in various scenarios.

An real life example for this that is useful is for cyber security, in digital storage, if you're hashing more passwords than available unique hashes, at least two passwords will collide.
